"The Grand Budapest Hotel" Review

“The Grand Budapest Hotel”, directed by Wes Anderson, is a quirky and meaningful comedy-drama film that not only perfectly exemplifies the unique style of films by the same director but also heavily underlines themes of human morality, friendship, and nostalgia.

The film involves multiple layers of narration, creating an intriguing atmosphere for the setting of the main story. The movie’s plot follows the adventures of the great concierge Gustave H. and his lobby boy, Zero Mustafa, as they navigate the worlds of the affluent, the imprisoned, and the runaway fugitive amidst all the drama involving a dead dowager and a famous painting.

What truly makes this film stand out is its symmetrical style and uniquely vibrant color palette unlike most cinematic works nowadays, blended seamlessly with the whimsical storyline and witty dialogue, creating a truly magnificent viewer experience.

Beyond this first impression, the film’s melancholic depth also facilitates feelings of grief, nostalgia, and bittersweet as we are reminded of an era long past, with the hotel itself as its symbol.
